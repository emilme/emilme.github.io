About

Policy program associate at New America's Open Technology Institute.

I like to think about the Internet, cities, and food.

Before joining New America, I was a Fulbright Scholar in South Korea, doing fieldwork on the social and economic impacts (of what is sometimes called) the sharing economy in Seoul, and covering the Korean efforts to seed a startup ecosystem. I graduated from Yale University with a BA in Environmental Studies, and wrote my undergraduate thesis on how race and the built environment contribute to the digital divide. I previously worked with the Berkman Center for Internet and Society's Chilling Effects project, and helped build SF Open Law for the Mayor's Office of Civic Innovation in San Francisco.
